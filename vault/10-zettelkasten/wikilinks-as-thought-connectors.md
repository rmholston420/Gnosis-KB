# Wikilinks as Thought Connectors

A wikilink (`[[Note Title]]`) is not just navigation — it is a claim. When you write `[[Impermanence]]` inside a note about grief, you are asserting that these two ideas are related in a way worth preserving. The link is a micro-argument.

## The Serendipity Effect

The real value of wikilinks appears when browsing the backlinks of an old note and finding an unexpected connection made months ago. This is the Zettelkasten's primary gift: *emergent insight* — connections you did not consciously intend but that are real once surfaced.

## Linking Strategies

- **Link on concepts, not topics.** Link `[[First-Principles Thinking]]`, not `[[Physics]]`.
- **Link forward and backward.** When you create a new note, go back to old notes and add links pointing to it.
- **Broken links are prompts.** A red wikilink pointing to a note that doesn't exist yet is a reminder that the idea needs elaboration.

## Wikilinks in Gnosis

In Gnosis, every `[[Title]]` in a note body is parsed at sync time and stored as a directed edge in the link graph. This graph powers:
- The [[Maps of Content]] navigation
- The force-directed graph visualisation
- The AI suggest-links feature, which finds notes that *should* be linked but aren't
