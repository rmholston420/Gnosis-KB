# Emergence and Complexity

Emergence refers to properties or behaviours that arise at higher levels of organisation but **cannot be predicted or explained solely by the properties of lower-level components**. Water's liquidity is not a property of individual H₂O molecules. Consciousness is not a property of individual neurons.

## Weak vs. Strong Emergence

**Weak emergence**: the higher-level property is in principle reducible — it could be derived from the lower-level description with sufficient computational power. Most cases in physics and chemistry are of this type.

**Strong emergence**: the higher-level property is *irreducible* — it cannot even in principle be derived from the lower-level description. Consciousness is the most debated candidate.

## Complexity Science

Complex adaptive systems (CAS) — studied at the Santa Fe Institute — share several features:
- Many heterogeneous agents interacting locally
- No central controller
- Adaptive behaviour: agents respond to feedback from the environment
- Self-organisation: global patterns emerge from local rules
- Edge of chaos: maximal complexity and adaptability near the boundary between order and disorder

## Relevance to Knowledge Systems

A [[zettelkasten-method|Zettelkasten]] is arguably a complex adaptive system. The "agents" are notes; the interactions are [[knowledge-graph-as-mind-map|wikilinks]]; the emergent property is **insight** — arguments and patterns that no individual note contains. The researcher acts as an adaptive agent in the system, adding and linking notes in response to what the system surfaces.
