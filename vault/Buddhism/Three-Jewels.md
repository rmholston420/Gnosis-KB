# The Three Jewels

The Three Jewels (*Triratna*) — the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha — form the foundational refuge of Buddhist practice.  Taking refuge in the Three Jewels is the entry point into the Buddhist path for practitioners of all vehicles.

## The Buddha

The Buddha (*Skt. Buddha*, Tib. *Sangye*) represents the fully awakened mind: the union of wisdom (*jnana*) and compassion (*karuna*).  In Vajrayana context this includes all Buddhas of the three times and ten directions, and ultimately one's own buddha-nature (*tathagatagarbha*).

## The Dharma

The Dharma (*Chos*) refers both to the teachings (*Sutras*, *Tantras*) and to the path of realization itself — liberation from samsara through insight into the nature of mind.  In Tibetan tradition the Dharma is further divided into:
- **Transmission Dharma** (*lung gi chos*) — the scriptural canon
- **Realization Dharma** (*rtogs pa'i chos*) — direct insight

## The Sangha

The Sangha (*dGe 'dun*) means the community of practitioners.  In the highest sense it refers to the Arya Sangha — those who have directly realized emptiness (*shunyata*).  In the relative sense it includes all fellow practitioners supporting each other on the path.
