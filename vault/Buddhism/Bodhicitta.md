# Bodhicitta

*Bodhicitta* (Tib. *Byang chub kyi sems* — "awakening mind") is the aspiration to attain full Buddhahood for the benefit of all sentient beings without exception.  It is the seed of the Mahayana and Vajrayana paths and the most powerful antidote to self-cherishing.

## Two Aspects

### Relative Bodhicitta

Relative bodhicitta has two aspects:
1. **Aspiring Bodhicitta** (*mön pa'i sems bskyed*) — the wish to attain enlightenment for all beings
2. **Engaging Bodhicitta** (*'jug pa'i sems bskyed*) — actually practicing the six paramitas as the means

These are compared to wishing to travel somewhere and actually setting out on the journey.

### Absolute Bodhicitta

Absolute bodhicitta (*don dam byang chub kyi sems*) is the direct recognition of mind's empty, luminous nature — *rigpa* in Dzogchen terminology.  This is not a mental attitude but a state of non-conceptual awareness.

## The Six Paramitas

Engaging bodhicitta is expressed through the six transcendent virtues (*paramitas*):
1. Generosity (*dana*)
2. Ethical discipline (*sila*)
3. Patience (*kshanti*)
4. Joyful effort (*virya*)
5. Meditative concentration (*dhyana*)
6. Wisdom (*prajna*)

## Tonglen Practice

Tonglen ("giving and taking") is a meditation for cultivating relative bodhicitta: on the in-breath taking in the suffering of others; on the out-breath sending happiness and well-being.
