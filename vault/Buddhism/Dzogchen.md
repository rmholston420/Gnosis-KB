# Dzogchen — The Great Perfection

Dzogchen (*rDzogs pa chen po*, "Great Perfection" or "Great Completion") is the pinnacle teaching of the Nyingma school of Tibetan Buddhism.  It points directly to the nature of mind — *rigpa* — without the gradual path of renunciation and transformation characteristic of Sutrayana and lower Tantra.

## Rigpa

*Rigpa* (awareness, pure presence) is the ground-state of mind: empty (*kadak*, primordially pure) yet luminously aware (*lhundrup*, spontaneously present).  Confusion arises when this awareness does not recognize itself and grasps at arising experiences as solid and external.  Liberation is *recognition* (*ngo shes pa*) — not attainment of a new state but seeing what was always already the case.

## Three Series

Dzogchen is traditionally taught in three series:

1. **Semde** (Mind Series) — emphasizes the nature of mind as the ground
2. **Longde** (Space Series) — emphasizes the vast expanse (*klong*) of awareness
3. **Mennagde** (Instruction Series, Upadesha) — the most direct pointing-out instructions; includes *Trekchö* (cutting through) and *Tögal* (direct crossing-over)

## Trekchö and Tögal

- **Trekchö** (*Khregs chod*) — "cutting through" solidified grasping; resting in the naked, unelaborated state of rigpa; related to [[Shunyata]]
- **Tögal** (*Thod rgal*) — "direct crossing"; working with the spontaneous visions (*nyams*) arising in practice; related to luminosity

## The View in Daily Life

Dzogchen is not only formal sitting meditation.  The view (*lta ba*) is to maintain recognition of rigpa in all activities — walking, eating, sleeping — so that every experience becomes self-liberation in the *dharmata*.
