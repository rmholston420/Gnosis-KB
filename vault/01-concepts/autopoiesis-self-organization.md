# Autopoiesis and Self-Organization

Maturana and Varela coined *autopoiesis* (self-making) to describe living systems: an autopoietic system continuously produces the components that constitute it, while those components maintain the network of processes that produce them.

A cell is autopoietic. A whirlpool is *not* — it maintains its structure but does not produce its own components. The distinction marks the boundary between the merely self-organizing and the *living*.

## The Cognitive Dimension

Maturana's radical move: **cognition is not representation**. A living system does not build an internal model of an external world and then act on that model. Instead, *cognition is the act of bringing forth a world* through the organism's structural coupling with its environment.

This dissolves the traditional subject-object split and aligns with the Yogācāra Buddhist analysis of how experience arises: not a pre-given world perceived by a pre-given mind, but co-arising.

## Connection to Emptiness

If living systems bring forth rather than represent their worlds, then the world has no intrinsic properties independent of the observer — which resonates with Madhyamaka's claim that phenomena lack inherent existence and are constituted by *designation* (Tib. *btags pa*) relative to a cognitive system.
