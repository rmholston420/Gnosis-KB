# First Encounter with Śūnyatā

Today's morning sitting: the breath dissolving into awareness dissolving into — what exactly? Not nothingness. The Tibetan term *stong pa nyid* translates as "emptiness" but carries the connotation of *full potential*, not vacuum.

Nāgārjuna's key move in the *Mūlamadhyamakakārikā* is demonstrating that things lack *svabhāva* (inherent existence) not by showing they don't exist, but by showing they exist *dependently* — always already in relation.

**Question for investigation:** Is the emptiness I encounter in meditation the same emptiness that Madhyamaka philosophy describes analytically, or are these two different access points to the same territory?
