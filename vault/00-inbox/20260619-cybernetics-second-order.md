# Second-Order Cybernetics — The Observer Enters the System

Heinz von Foerster's crucial shift: first-order cybernetics studies *observed systems*; second-order cybernetics studies *observing systems* — including the scientist as part of the system being described.

This resonates deeply with the Madhyamaka critique of subject-object duality. In both frameworks, the notion of a detached, view-from-nowhere observer is shown to be untenable.

**Key claim:** Von Foerster's "Eigen-behaviors" — stable behaviors a system converges to under recursive operation — may be a formal analog to what contemplative traditions call *rigpa* (pure awareness), which is self-stabilizing rather than constructed.
